Muhammad Shaikh's Summer 2025 Work Term Report

Introduction

Hi there! My name is Muhammad Shaikh. During the Summer semester of 2025, I worked remotely as a Software Engineer for DataInn Inc., a company that builds SaaS-based solutions for businesses. I contributed to AutismResourceHub, a online social platform designed to help connect families who have children with autism to support centres and programs. My work focused on resolving critical bugs, developing new features, and optimizing the performance of the application. I collaborated with a distributed team using Microsoft Teams and other tools, ensuring a seamless workflow and a consistent approach to development. This experience helped me grow both technically and professionally, particularly by working with React, Typescript, Node.js, Redux, PostgreSQL, and Drizzle ORM.

Employer

DataInn Inc. is a leading technology company based in Toronto, Canada, specializing in SaaS-based solutions for businesses. The company develops software solutions that help organizations streamline operations, improve efficiency, and meet industry-specific needs. One of their primary offerings is SimpleAccounts, a software designed to simplify business accounting tasks such as managing invoices, credit notes, debit notes, journals, and other core financial operations. The team I worked with was remote, with colleagues spread across various locations, but the company's headquarters is based in Toronto. SimpleAccounts is marketed directly through their website, simpleaccounts.io, and is trusted by businesses of all sizes to manage their accounting needs.

Goals

I also contributed to deploying code and updates to the cloud. When I encountered obstacles, I took initiative by searching for solutions online or reaching out to more experienced team members for guidance. This approach not only helped me overcome technical challenges but also accelerated my learning. By the end of the term, I felt significantly more confident in my ability to use these technologies effectively and independently.

Learning Goal 4:

I was proud to take on a leadership role during my project, acting as the lead developer responsible for a wide range of tasks from fixing bugs and developing new features to testing and deployment. Job Description

In my role as a Software Engineer, I worked across both the frontend and backend of a social platform called AutismResourceHub, designed to help families in the autism community connect with resources, support groups, and local events. The tech stack included React, TypeScript, Node.js, Redux, PostgreSQL, and Drizzle ORM. I also leveraged a custom Replit Agent to assist in developing core platform features—acting as both a system designer and tester to ensure smooth functionality across both the user-facing interface and the developer experience. Key features I implemented included user registration for events, discussion forums, article publishing, resource sharing, user profile creation, and document uploads. I ensured these features were not only functionally complete but also robust through thorough validation, testing, and performance optimization. Additionally, I addressed critical bugs—such as data formatting issues and validation errors—and contributed to a scalable architecture. Working in a remote environment, I collaborated closely with the team during sprint planning, participated in code reviews, and played an active role in maintaining development quality and velocity.

Conclusions

In conclusion, my work on Autism ResourceHub significantly contributed to my growth as a full-stack developer. I was deeply involved in building meaningful features for a socially impactful platform, while also addressing critical bugs and ensuring high performance and usability. By leveraging technologies like React, TypeScript, Node.js, PostgreSQL, and Drizzle ORM—along with a custom Replit Agent—I not only implemented features but also took on system design and testing responsibilities to ensure a seamless experience for both users and developers. This role strengthened my technical skill set, deepened my understanding of full-stack development workflows, and enhanced my collaboration and communication skills in a remote, team-driven environment. Overall, the experience shaped me into a more well-rounded and thoughtful software engineer.
